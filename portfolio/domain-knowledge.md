## Industry Context
Salesforce implementations run through partners (like Publicis Sapient) who sit between Salesforce and end clients. Projects are scrum-based, story-driven, and delivery is measured against sprint velocity. Government clients add layers of compliance, approval, and process rigour that commercial clients typically don't have.

## Frameworks and Mental Models
- **Agile/Scrum:** Sprints, ceremonies, story points, backlog grooming — this is the default operating model.
- **MVP-first:** Ship the minimum viable version, iterate from there. Don't over-engineer before validating.
- **Test-driven development:** Tests written alongside or before code. Coverage is non-negotiable.
- **Separation of concerns:** Logic, data, and presentation layers kept distinct.
- **Shift left:** Catch issues earlier in the delivery pipeline — in design and refinement, not in UAT.
- **Best tool for the job:** No dogma about declarative vs. code. If Apex performs better, use Apex. Pick the right tool for the context.
