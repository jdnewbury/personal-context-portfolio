# Decision Log

## How I Make Decisions
Analytical and consultative. Default approach is to gather the relevant information, map out pros, cons, and tradeoffs, and talk it through with the right people before committing. Not impulsive — but not paralysed either. When the information runs out, a gut call is made and monitored.

## What I Need Before Deciding
- A clear view of the pros and cons.
- An understanding of the tradeoffs — what's gained, what's given up.
- Where relevant, a sense of what external factors (other people's behaviour, market conditions, risk environment) are likely to do to the options.

## Recent Decisions

### Backup Flight to the UK
**The decision:** Whether to purchase a backup flight to the UK given instability in the Middle East.

**How it was thought through:** Weighed the pros and cons of buying vs. not buying. Considered the risk environment — what the conflict was likely to do to flight availability and pricing. Factored in what other travellers were likely to do in response to current events and how that would affect the cost of alternatives if the primary flight was disrupted.

**Outcome:** Decision made based on risk assessment and likely market behaviour — not gut feel alone, but a reasoned read of the situation.

## How I Handle Uncertainty
- **Missing requirements before scoping:** Request the information. Don't scope on incomplete foundations.
- **Sprint blockers:** Escalate to the scrum master. Don't absorb blockers that should be escalated.
- **Unclear implementation direction:** Give a direction based on information to hand, but explicitly flag that new information may change the call. The direction is provisional, not final.
- **Technology or tool choice without full evaluation:** Make a gut call and see how it pans out. Pick a direction, move, and adjust.

## Who I Consult
Consultative by nature — talk it through with the right people before deciding. Looking for information and a sounding board on tradeoffs, not necessarily validation or challenge for its own sake.
