# Role and Responsibilities
**Name:** Jason Newbury
**Organization:** Publicis Sapient — Salesforce Practice

## Core Responsibilities
Translating business requirements into technically actionable Jira stories for a Salesforce implementation at a federal government agency. This means taking incoming business user stories, refining them with the solution designer, adding technical acceptance criteria, and getting them to a point where the development team can pick them up and execute. If developers hit blockers or need information to move forward, that's on me to resolve.

## Weekly Cadence
- **Daily stand-up** Mon–Thu at 10am (no Friday stand-up)
- **Business story walkthrough** — weekly session reviewing incoming stories from the business
- **Solution design document walkthrough** — weekly review of solution design with the solution designer
- **Story intake** — pulling Jira stories assigned to the scrum team into the squad board
- **Story refinement session** — working with the solution designer to refine stories and add technical acceptance criteria
- **Estimation session** — sitting with the scrum team to review and estimate refined stories
- **Developer unblocking** — ongoing throughout the week; fielding questions and sourcing information to keep developers moving

## Monthly / Quarterly Rhythms
Driven by sprint and scrum cadence on a federal government project. No additional monthly or quarterly rhythms noted.

## Key Decisions
- Who to go to and where to source information when the development team is blocked
- How to translate a business requirement into a well-scoped, technically accurate Jira story

## What I Produce
Refined Jira tickets — business stories translated into technical stories with clear acceptance criteria, ready for developer assignment and execution.
